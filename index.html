The Plan

How this trip goes

We land. We head to the park. We enjoy every minute of it. Then Sunday we race.

01
We Land & Head to the Park

Fly into Seattle-Tacoma September 16th or 17th, grab a rental, and drive straight toward the Cascades. Mount Rainier is about 2 hours from the airport. The mountain starts appearing through the windshield about halfway there. It's massive. Nothing prepares you for it.

02
Hikes, Sunrises & Sunsets

Days in the park are ours — Skyline Loop, Reflection Lakes at dawn, Naches Peak, Myrtle Falls. September means the meadows are red and gold, the trails are empty, and the light is incredible. Early morning at Reflection Lakes with Rainier doubled in still water is something you remember forever.

03
We Have an Absolute Blast

This isn't a grueling outdoors trip — it's a beautiful one. Cool crisp air, almost no crowds, wildlife everywhere, views that don't look real. We eat well, sleep well, and soak in one of the most spectacular places in the country.

04
Sunday Funday — Spartan Race

Sunday September 20th we drive ~50 miles to Monroe and race the Spartan Super 10K. Mud, obstacles, evergreen forest trails, Cascade Mountain backdrop. After everything we've seen that weekend, crossing that finish line is going to feel even better. Fly home September 20th or 21st — your call.

Why This Timing is Perfect

Mid-September is the sweet spot

Everyone visits Rainier in July and August. We're going when it's actually at its most beautiful — and most peaceful.

01
Fall Colors Peak

Mid-September marks the start of fall at Rainier. Huckleberry fields turn deep crimson, vine maples go orange and gold, and the meadows at Paradise and Sunrise blaze with color against the white snowcap.

02
Crowds Disappear

After Labor Day the summer visitors vanish. The same trails that had packed parking lots in August are nearly empty. You'll have iconic hikes like the Skyline Loop almost entirely to yourself.

03
All Trails Open

Every major trail — Paradise, Sunrise, Chinook Pass, Wonderland — is fully accessible in mid-September. By late October many high-elevation routes close for snow. This is the last sweet window before winter.

04
Perfect Weather

Daytime highs around 60°F at lower elevations — crisp, clear, and ideal for hiking. September is one of the drier months in the park. Cool enough to feel like fall, warm enough to spend all day outside.

05
Wildlife Active

Animals are stocking up for winter and are more visible than any other time of year. Marmots, mountain goats, deer, and elk are commonly spotted on September trails — especially at Sunrise.

06
No Reservations

No timed entry reservations needed in 2026. Just show up. The $30 vehicle pass covers 7 days in the park — or grab an America the Beautiful annual pass for $80 and visit as many parks as you want all year.

What the Park Has to Offer

Trails, waterfalls & views

Something for every level. All of these are at their most beautiful and least crowded in mid-September.

Skyline Loop Trail
Paradise · 5.5 miles · Moderate-Hard

The crown jewel. Loops above the treeline through meadows that turn red and orange in September, with unobstructed summit views the entire way. John Muir called Paradise "the most luxuriant and most extravagantly beautiful of all the alpine gardens I ever beheld."

Naches Peak Loop
Chinook Pass · 3 miles · Easy-Moderate

One of the most picture-perfect short hikes in the park. Huckleberry meadows, Tipsoo Lake views, and Rainier looming overhead the whole way. One of the best fall color hikes in Washington state.

Reflection Lakes
Paradise Corridor · Easy Walk

At dawn the lakes go glass-still and Rainier's full 14,411-foot profile doubles in the water. One of the most photographed spots in the Pacific Northwest — hits completely different in person.

Myrtle Falls
Paradise · 0.4 miles · Easy

Short walk from the Paradise visitor center to a stunning waterfall with Rainier framed directly behind it. One of the most iconic photo spots in any national park in the country.

Burroughs Mountain
Sunrise · 4.7–7 miles · Moderate

Sweeping views of Rainier's north face from tundra terrain. Marmots everywhere in September. Choose First or Second Burroughs depending on how your legs feel. Worth every step.

Summerland Trail
Sunrise · 10.1 miles · Moderate

Climbs to an open meadow blazing with fall foliage with Rainier's summit towering above. One of the best all-day hikes in the park. Almost no one else on the trail after Labor Day.
