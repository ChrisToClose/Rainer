Why September

The mountain at its most alive

Mid-September is Rainier's best-kept secret. The summer crowds are gone, the alpine meadows ignite with fall color — deep reds, burnt oranges, gold — and the snowcap glows against crisp blue skies. Most people never see it like this.

On the Mountain

Trails worth every step

Rainier has trails for every level. In mid-September the fall color is peaking and you'll have the paths mostly to yourself.

Skyline Loop Trail
Paradise Area

The classic Rainier hike. Stunning fall foliage, panoramic views of the summit, and sweeping sightlines of the Tatoosh Range. Tough elevation gain, absolutely worth it.

Alta Vista Trail
Paradise Area

A shorter loop through meadows in full autumn color. Red huckleberry and vine maple against the white snowcap — this is the postcard view of Rainier.

Reflection Lakes
Paradise Corridor

At dawn the lakes go perfectly still and Rainier doubles in the water. One of the most photographed spots in the Pacific Northwest — and completely different in real life.

Sunrise Area Trails
Northeast Rainier · 6,400 ft

The highest point accessible by car in the park. The views from here stretch across the entire Cascade Range. September means golden larch and almost zero crowds.
